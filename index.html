About

I am a game design analyst and indie developer with a focus on psychological horror. My work starts not with engines or tools but with why fear works — the mechanical, narrative, and sonic systems that make players feel something they can't explain.

My primary research project is a 35-page deep analysis of Cry of Fear — examining enemy design, pacing architecture, narrative mechanics, and how a free Half-Life mod created one of the most psychologically resonant horror experiences ever made.

Currently building a structured AI prompt toolkit for indie horror developers — 50 complete design briefs drawn from the same analytical framework, built to generate implementation-ready documentation.

Destination: Japan. The long game.

Goals

  • Ship a complete indie horror game
  • Build a public game design portfolio
  • Work at a Japanese game studio
  • Master game design as a discipline
  • Learn Japanese to N3
  • Document what makes horror work
