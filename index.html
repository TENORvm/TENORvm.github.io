About

I am a game design analyst with a focus on horror and action-RPG titles. My work begins not with tools or engines but with a single question: why does this work? — the mechanical, narrative, and systemic choices that make certain games impossible to forget.

My published design documents include a 35-page deep analysis of Cry of Fear — examining enemy psychology, pacing architecture, level design philosophy, and how a free Half-Life mod became one of the most psychologically resonant horror experiences ever created — and a full analysis of Amnesia: The Bunker, exploring survival tension, resource scarcity, and systemic fear design.

Upcoming analyses span the Dead Space series, Silent Hill series, Resident Evil series, Amnesia series, Dark Souls series, and selected non-horror titles where design craft reaches the same level of intentionality. The scope is not limited to horror — it is limited to games that mean something.

Currently open to work — seeking a position at an international game studio where design is taken seriously as a discipline. Destination: abroad. The long game.

Design Concept

An experimental psychological thriller disguised as a calm birdwatching simulator. Observation replaces action, and tension emerges from silence, repetition, and subtle inconsistencies. There is no direct horror — only the slow realization that something is wrong. Player choices lead to multiple endings, including a hidden truth that reframes the entire experience.
