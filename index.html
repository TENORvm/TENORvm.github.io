About

I am a game design analyst with a focus on horror and action-RPG titles. My work begins not with tools or engines but with a single question: why does this work? — the mechanical, narrative, and systemic choices that make certain games impossible to forget.

My first published design document is a 35-page deep analysis of Cry of Fear — examining enemy psychology, pacing architecture, level design philosophy, and how a free Half-Life mod became one of the most psychologically resonant horror experiences ever created. It is the beginning of a larger body of work.

Upcoming analyses span the Dead Space series, Silent Hill series, Resident Evil series, Amnesia series (currently playing Amnesia: The Bunker), Dark Souls series, and selected non-horror titles where design craft reaches the same level of intentionality. The scope is not limited to horror — it is limited to games that mean something.

Currently open to work — seeking a position at an international game studio where design is taken seriously as a discipline. Destination: abroad. The long game.
